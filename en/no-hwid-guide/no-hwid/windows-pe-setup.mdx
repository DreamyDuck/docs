<Note>
      - This step is recommended for all users who require completely clean,
      trace-free windows
      - This step require an USB storage drive with atleast
      16GB and USB 3.0
    </Note>
<Steps>
  <Step title="Plug in your USB Storage Drive">
    > Make sure you don't have any important files inside.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install Ventroy into your USBc Storage Drive">
    1. Download **Ventroy** Installer from Official Site. (Link on top)
    <Frame>
    <img src="/en/images/win-pe0.png" />
    <img src="/en/images/win-pe1.png" />
    </Frame>
    2. Unzip the downloaded zip file.
    3. Open the folder and run `Ventroy2Disk.exe`.
    4. Choose the plugged USB Storage Drive and Click `Install`.
    5. After installation complete, unplug and plug in again the USB storage drive. **A new storage `Ventroy` will now appear as empty usb drive. This storage will be use to store all our needed files.**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare all requires files">
    <Warning>This step is important; you may lose internet connection in WinPE. Therefore, prepare all the required files on the USB.</Warning>
    1. Download the **Windows ISO** and **All Files** in our Cloud Drive. (Link on top)
    2. Place all the files into `Ventroy Storage (USB Storage)`.
   </Step>
   <Step title="Boot into Windows PE">
        #### Method 1
        > 1. Shutdown your PC and power on. During startup, press <kbd>F12</kbd> or <kbd>F12</kbd> or <kbd>F12</kbd> or <kbd>F12</kbd> or <kbd>F12</kbd> to access the boot menu.  
        **(Different brands of motherboards have different trigger buttons, but pressing all the buttons together can activate them.)**
        > 2. In the boot selection, choose the usb drive (ventroy)

        #### Method 2
        > 1. Press and holding <kbd>SHIFT</kbd> button and click restart. (Don't release the `SHIFT` button until it load into troubleshoot page)
        > <Frame><img src="/en/images/win-pe2.png" /></Frame>
        > 2. It will load into Blue Options Page.
        > 3. Choose `Troubleshoot` -> `Advanced Optoins` -> `Startup Settings`. It will now help you boot into BIOS.
        > 4. Find `Boot` option, choose the usb drive (ventroy) and boot into it.
        <Warning>
            If you encounter a prompt indicating that security is preventing booting from USB, please follow this video to disable Secure Boot. -> https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Tr9RUo58--0
        </Warning>
        </Step>
        <Step title="Select HBCD_PE.x64.iso in Ventory booting selection">
        > 5. After successfully boot into Ventroy, choose the previous downloaded `HBCD_PE.x64.iso`.
        > 6. It will now boot into the Windows PE system.
        </Step>
</Steps>
